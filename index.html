Why Sleep
Matters For Students

Sleep is one of the most important parts of a healthy lifestyle. Getting enough sleep helps students improve their grades, reduce stress, increase energy levels and stay focused throughout the school day.

Learn More Sleep Tips

Benefits of Sleep

Getting enough sleep improves every part of your life. From learning in class to feeling happier throughout the day, sleep gives your brain and body the chance to recover and perform at their best.

Better Grades

Sleep improves memory, concentration and problem solving. When you get 8–10 hours of sleep your brain stores information more effectively, making tests and assignments easier.

Less Stress

A good night's sleep helps regulate emotions and lowers stress. Students who are well rested are more likely to stay calm, manage challenges and maintain a positive mood.

More Energy

Sleep allows your body to recover and recharge. With enough rest you'll feel more energetic during school, sports and other daily activities.

Sleep Facts

Scientists recommend teenagers get between 8 and 10 hours of sleep every night. Sleep is essential for brain development, learning and overall health.

Brain Power

While you sleep your brain processes everything you learned during the day. This helps improve memory, concentration and learning.

8–10 Hours

Most teenagers need between eight and ten hours of quality sleep every night to perform their best at school and stay healthy.

Healthy Body

Sleep strengthens your immune system, repairs muscles and supports healthy growth during your teenage years.

Better Mood

Students who get enough sleep often feel happier, calmer and are better at handling stressful situations.

More Performance

Whether you're playing sports or exercising, getting enough sleep improves reaction time, endurance and coordination.

Learning

Being well rested helps you pay attention in class, understand lessons and complete homework more effectively.

Simple Ways To Sleep Better

Small habits every day can help you get better quality sleep and wake up feeling refreshed.

1

Keep A Schedule

Try going to bed and waking up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

2

Put Your Phone Away

Avoid screens for at least 30 minutes before bed because blue light can make it harder to fall asleep.

3

Create A Relaxing Bedroom

Keep your room cool, dark and quiet to help your body relax naturally.

4

Avoid Caffeine

Energy drinks, coffee and fizzy drinks late in the day can keep you awake for hours.

5

Exercise During The Day

Regular physical activity helps you fall asleep faster and improves sleep quality.
